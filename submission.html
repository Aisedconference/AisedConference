Paper submission.

Researchers, academics, postgraduate students, policymakers, industry practitioners and professionals are invited to submit original and unpublished papers related to the conference theme and thematic tracks.

Conference theme

Harnessing AI, Empowering Innovation, Advancing Sustainable Futures Through Entrepreneurship and Diplomacy.

Track 1AI for Sustainable Development

Exploring how AI can address global sustainability challenges and accelerate the achievement of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Track 2Sustainable Entrepreneurship and Innovation

Innovative business models, startup ecosystems, social enterprises and sustainable value creation in the digital economy.

Track 3AI and Digital Transformation in Business

Emerging technologies, automation, digital leadership, smart enterprises and AI-driven organisational transformation.

Track 4Diplomacy in the AI Era

The evolving role of diplomacy, international relations and global cooperation in an increasingly AI-powered world.

Track 5Policy, Governance and Ethics of AI

Responsible AI, governance frameworks, data ethics, cybersecurity, trust, regulation and public policy considerations.

Track 6Green Economy and Circular Business Models

Sustainable industries, circular economy strategies, ESG practices and green innovation for future economies.

Track 7Education, Skills and AI for Future Work

Future-ready education systems, lifelong learning, micro-credentials, workforce transformation and digital talent development.

Track 8Smart Cities and Sustainable Societies

Urban innovation, smart infrastructure, digital governance and technology-enabled sustainable communities.

Track 9AI for Health, Well-being and Human Security

AI applications in healthcare, mental well-being, public health systems and human-centred technological solutions.

Track 10Global Collaboration and Future Diplomacy

Cross-border partnerships, international development, strategic alliances and collaborative innovation ecosystems.

Guidelines

Submission guidelines.

Abstract length

Abstracts should be between 250 - 300 words.

Full paper length

Full papers should be between 4,000 - 6,000 words.

Language

Papers must be written in English.

Originality

All submissions must be original, unpublished and not under consideration by other conferences or journals.

Review process

Submissions will undergo a peer-review and editorial evaluation process.

Track alignment

Authors are encouraged to align papers with the conference thematic tracks and interdisciplinary focus areas.

Publication opportunities

Selected papers will be considered for publication opportunities in SCOPUS-indexed journals, MyCITE-indexed journals and other international peer-reviewed publications.

Further updates

Paper templates, review guidelines and publication opportunities will be announced through official conference communication channels.
